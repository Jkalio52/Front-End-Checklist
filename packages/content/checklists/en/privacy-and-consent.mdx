Privacy failures are usually integration failures: analytics, embeds, forms, storage, and consent banners all need to work together. This checklist gives you a focused pass on the browser-visible controls and policies that affect user trust and regulatory exposure.

## Who It's For

- Teams shipping analytics, embeds, personalization, or lead forms
- Reviewers validating browser-visible consent and privacy behavior
- Launch owners checking trust-sensitive flows before release

## Done Looks Like

You should finish knowing whether consent gates, storage behavior, privacy policy copy, and third-party scripts actually align in the browser.

## What To Review

- Whether consent choices actually gate tracking and third-party behavior
- Whether you are collecting more data than the feature genuinely needs
- Whether the user-facing privacy policy matches what the frontend does in practice
- Whether form submissions and browser headers protect personal data in transit

## Best Uses

- Before launching new marketing, analytics, or personalization tooling
- During regional compliance reviews
- After adding signup flows, lead forms, or embedded third-party widgets

## Review Tips

1. Test the site both before and after giving consent.
2. Inspect network requests and storage keys, not only the banner UI.
3. Treat third-party scripts as part of the privacy review scope, not an exception to it.
